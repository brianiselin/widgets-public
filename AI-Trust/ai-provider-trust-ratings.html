Methodology v1.0

A grade is not a verdict on how good a model is at its job. It is a measure of what happens to your data, who can reach it, and what legal or commercial pressure sits behind the company providing it. Click a card to see the breakdown; open the assessment for the sourcing behind each score.

Grades: A = low risk  →  E = high risk, per category
How to read this. Each provider is scored across four aggregate categories, each built from two underlying factors. A category grade is the weaker of its two factors, not an average — a strong score on one factor does not cancel a weak score on the other. Where public information was insufficient to score a factor, that is marked rather than guessed. Investor relationships and concentration-of-control facts (for example, a shared investor between an AI company and its verification vendor, or a single individual holding unchecked operational control) are scored where they affect a factor's grade and explained in full in the written assessment, rather than added as a separate bar — these are disclosed facts about structure, not character judgements about named individuals. This is a working rubric, not a regulatory finding, and individual scores should be read alongside the sourcing in each assessment panel.
