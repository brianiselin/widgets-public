Timeline of Japan’s Taiwan Turn (2015–2025)
From security laws and island clashes to Ukraine, new strategy documents and Taiwan polling, a decade pushed Japan towards the frontline.
2015 2020 2025
2015 – Security Laws Passed
Diet approves Legislation for Peace and Security, enabling limited collective self-defence under reinterpreted Article 9.
2016–2017 – Senkaku Flashpoints
Chinese coast guard and fishing vessels surge around the Senkaku Islands, turning grey-zone pressure into a constant.
2019–2020 – Hong Kong Crackdown
Beijing imposes a national security law on Hong Kong and crushes dissent, eroding trust in “one country, two systems”.
Feb 2022 – Russia Invades Ukraine
Full-scale war in Europe shocks Tokyo and hardens debates about deterrence, alliances and a Taiwan contingency.
Dec 2022 – New Defence Strategy
Japan adopts a new National Security Strategy and three defence documents, planning to lift spending towards 2% of GDP.
Nov 2025 – Takaichi’s Taiwan Line
Prime minister tells the Diet that Chinese force against Taiwan could be a “survival-threatening situation” for Japan.
Nov 2025 – Kyodo Taiwan Poll
Kyodo finds 48.8% support using collective self-defence if China attacks Taiwan, with 60.4% backing faster defence build-up.
