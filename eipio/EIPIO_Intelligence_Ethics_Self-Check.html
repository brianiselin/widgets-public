EIPIO Ethics Self-Check
Core principles for democratic intelligence practice
European Institute of Professional Intelligence Officers

This self-check helps you reflect on core ethical principles in intelligence work in democratic systems: legality, necessity and proportionality, human rights, professional integrity and accountability.

Sections A and B are scenario and knowledge questions (single best answer). Section C is a set of self-reflection statements; there are no “right” answers there.

Confidentiality note: this tool is designed for personal reflection. No data is sent anywhere; your responses stay in this browser session.

Section C – Self-Reflection Statements (Questions 21–30)

For each statement, rate yourself on a 1–5 scale: 1 = Strongly disagree, 2 = Disagree, 3 = Neither, 4 = Agree, 5 = Strongly agree. There are no right or wrong answers.
Q21. I understand the main legal authorities and constraints that govern my work and could explain them in plain language.
Q22. If I felt strong pressure to shape or soften an assessment for political reasons, I would know where and how to raise concerns.
Q23. I regularly pause to consider how my personal beliefs or biases might be influencing my judgements.
Q24. I feel able to say “no” when asked to do something that may be legal but feels ethically questionable.
Q25. I know which internal bodies (ethics, compliance, oversight, ombuds, etc.) I can approach if I have ethical concerns.
Q26. My team’s culture encourages open discussion of ethical dilemmas, not just technical or operational challenges.
Q27. I am confident that my organisation takes human rights and democratic accountability seriously in operational decisions.
Q28. When working with foreign partners, I consciously consider their human-rights record and legal framework, not just their utility.
Q29. I have, at least once in the past year, raised a concern or asked a challenging question about the ethical or legal side of a tasking, product or operation.
Q30. I have a personal line that I will not cross, even under pressure, and I have thought about what I would do if ordered to cross it.
